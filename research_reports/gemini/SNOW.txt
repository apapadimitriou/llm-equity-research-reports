Analyst Note

We are maintaining our $1,050 fair value estimate for wide-moat ServiceNow . The company continues to execute at a best-in-class level, solidifying its position as the essential orchestration layer for enterprise-wide digital transformation. The powerful secular tailwinds of workflow automation and generative AI adoption are expected to fuel durable, high-teens revenue growth and a steady expansion of operating margins for the foreseeable future . ServiceNow is scheduled to report its second-quarter 2025 financial results on July 23, 2025 . We anticipate another strong performance, with consensus estimates pointing to robust subscription revenue growth. This momentum is likely driven by accelerating demand for the company's AI-powered Now Assist solutions, which demonstrated significant traction in the first quarter and prompted strong forward guidance from management .
While ServiceNow's operational excellence is undisputed, its valuation remains demanding, trading at a significant premium to its software peers . The stock currently trades at a slight discount to our fair value estimate, suggesting it is fairly valued at present. The High Uncertainty rating reflects the stock's inherent sensitivity to any perceived execution missteps and the intensifying competitive landscape, particularly as it expands into new markets. ServiceNow offers a rare and compelling combination of durable growth and high profitability in the software sector. However, the premium valuation leaves little margin for error, warranting a neutral stance at the current price level.

Business Description

ServiceNow, Inc. is an American enterprise software company headquartered in Santa Clara, California . It provides a cloud-based platform-as-a-service (PaaS), known as the Now Platform, which enables large organizations to automate and manage digital workflows . The company's solutions are built upon a single code base and a unified data model, which serves as a key differentiator in the market .
The company's offerings are organized into four primary workflow categories :
Technology Workflows: This is the company's foundational and largest segment, comprising industry-leading solutions for IT Service Management (ITSM), IT Operations Management (ITOM), and IT Asset Management (ITAM) . These tools help organizations streamline IT service delivery, manage infrastructure, and optimize technology costs.
Customer Workflows: This segment includes solutions for Customer Service Management (CSM) and Field Service Management . These products aim to enhance customer experiences by connecting departments to resolve issues proactively and automate service requests.
Employee Workflows: This area focuses on improving the employee experience through products like HR Service Delivery (HRSD) and Workplace Service Delivery . These solutions automate processes for onboarding, HR requests, and facilities management.
Creator Workflows: This segment features a low-code application development platform, App Engine, which allows customers and partners to build custom applications to automate unique business processes without extensive coding knowledge .
While ServiceNow operates globally, North America remains its largest market, contributing the majority of its revenue .

Business Strategy & Outlook

ServiceNow's core competitive advantage is its single, unified Now Platform . Unlike many competitors that have grown through the acquisition of disparate technologies, ServiceNow has developed its broad suite of applications organically on a single code base. This architecture provides a seamless, integrated user experience and a common data model across all workflows. For customers, this simplifies the automation of complex, cross-departmental processes, enhances security and governance, and ultimately lowers the total cost of ownership by reducing the need for complex integrations between siloed systems.
The company's primary growth strategy is a "land-and-expand" model. ServiceNow typically establishes a foothold within a new customer account using its market-dominant ITSM solution. From this initial base, it systematically cross-sells additional workflows, such as Customer, Employee, and Creator solutions, along with premium features. The success of this strategy is evident in the consistent growth of its largest customer accounts. At the end of Q1 2025, the company reported having 508 customers with an annual contract value (ACV) exceeding $5 million, representing a 20% year-over-year increase .
The medium-term outlook is centered on the adoption of Generative AI. ServiceNow is aggressively embedding its "Now Assist" GenAI capabilities across its entire product portfolio, aiming to evolve its platform from a system of record to a system of action and intelligence . This strategy is designed to drive significant productivity gains for its customers by automating complex tasks, summarizing information, and generating code. Management has set an ambitious target of achieving $1 billion in ACV from Now Assist products by 2026, signaling that AI is the next major vector for growth and customer value creation. The strong uptake of these new offerings in Q1 2025, where Now Assist sales exceeded internal goals, provides early validation of this strategic pivot .

Bulls Say / Bears Say

Bulls Say: ServiceNow's wide economic moat is derived from exceptionally high customer switching costs, enabling it to maintain a 98% renewal rate and exercise strong pricing power. The Now Platform becomes deeply embedded in customer operations, making it prohibitively costly and operationally disruptive to replace. The platform is a primary beneficiary of two powerful secular trends: the digital transformation of the enterprise and the widespread adoption of generative AI . Its role as a mission-critical system for automating processes and boosting productivity positions it for durable long-term growth. The company's land-and-expand model provides a long runway for growth, with a substantial opportunity to cross-sell additional workflows and new AI-powered "Pro Plus" SKUs into its large and loyal installed base.
Bears Say: The stock trades at a significant valuation premium to the broader software industry and its direct peers on metrics such as forward price-to-earnings (approximately 52x) and enterprise value-to-sales (approximately 14x) . This elevated valuation assumes near-perfect execution and leaves the stock vulnerable to a sharp correction on any perceived slowdown in growth or margin expansion. As ServiceNow expands beyond its core IT market into areas like customer relationship management (CRM), it faces escalating competition from larger, well-entrenched rivals like Microsoft and Salesforce, both of which are also investing heavily in AI and automation. The company's growth is closely tied to enterprise IT spending, making it susceptible to macroeconomic downturns that could cause customers to delay large-scale digital transformation projects and scrutinize budgets .

Economic Moat

A wide economic moat is assigned to ServiceNow, rooted in high customer switching costs. The Now Platform often becomes the central nervous system for an enterprise's internal digital operations. Once customers have invested significant time and resources to build dozens of automated workflows, integrate numerous other enterprise systems (such as ERP and HCM), and train thousands of employees on the platform, the costs and risks associated with migrating to a competing solution become prohibitive. This is not merely a software replacement; it would entail a fundamental re-engineering of core business processes that have been built around the ServiceNow architecture.
Evidence of this powerful moat is abundant and consistent:
High Customer Retention: The company consistently reports a customer renewal rate of 98%, a figure that is among the best in the software industry and demonstrates the platform's extreme stickiness .
Deepening Customer Investment: The steady growth in large customer contracts is a clear indicator of the moat's strength. In Q2 2025, the company secured 89 deals valued at over $1 million in net new ACV. Furthermore, the number of customers with over $5 million in ACV grew to 528 by the end of the quarter . This shows that customers are not only staying on the platform but are also significantly increasing their investment over time.
Platform Ecosystem: The Creator Workflows and the ServiceNow Store foster a powerful ecosystem effect . As customers and partners build custom applications tailored to their specific needs, they further entrench the Now Platform within their operations. Each new custom application deployed effectively raises the switching costs, creating a virtuous cycle of dependency and investment.

Fair Value and Profit Drivers

The $1,050 fair value estimate is derived from a discounted cash flow (DCF) model. This valuation implies a forward fiscal 2025 enterprise value/sales multiple of 14 times and an adjusted price-to-earnings (P/E) multiple of 61 times .
The model's primary profit drivers are sustained revenue growth and gradual margin expansion.
Revenue Forecast: A five-year revenue compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of approximately 18% is modeled through 2029 . This is supported by analyst consensus estimates that project revenue will reach approximately $25.6 billion by 2029 . This growth is expected to be driven by continued market share gains in core ITSM, rapid expansion into adjacent markets such as customer service and HR, and strong customer adoption of new premium-priced, AI-infused products like the Now Assist Pro Plus SKUs.
Margin Expansion: Non-GAAP operating margins are projected to expand from the company's full-year 2025 guidance of 30.5% to approximately 32% by 2027 , with continued gradual expansion of 50-100 basis points annually thereafter . This margin improvement is driven by the significant operating leverage inherent in the company's single platform architecture and increasing internal productivity gains from the deployment of its own AI tools to automate internal processes .
EPS Bridge: The forecast growth in earnings per share, with consensus estimates reaching $32.59 by 2029 , is a direct result of the combination of strong top-line growth, operating leverage that drives margin expansion, and the modest but steady impact of the company's share repurchase program, which helps to reduce the outstanding share count over time.
The valuation is highly sensitive to these long-term growth assumptions. A material slowdown in the revenue growth rate would significantly impact the DCF-derived fair value, underscoring the high expectations embedded in the current stock price and justifying the High Uncertainty rating.

Risk & Uncertainty

The primary risk associated with ServiceNow is its premium valuation. The stock trades at high multiples of sales and earnings, reflecting optimistic growth expectations . Such stocks are particularly sensitive to shifts in investor sentiment and changes in the macroeconomic environment, especially interest rates. Any failure to meet these high expectations could trigger a significant de-rating of the stock.
The company operates in a fiercely competitive landscape. While its wide moat in core ITSM is formidable, its expansion into adjacent markets brings it into direct conflict with larger, well-entrenched competitors.
Hyperscalers: Microsoft poses a significant threat by leveraging its vast enterprise footprint to bundle competing products in areas like IT management and low-code development .
SaaS Specialists: Salesforce is the dominant player in CRM, a key strategic growth area for ServiceNow, and is also investing heavily in its own AI and automation capabilities .
Legacy Vendors: Companies like BMC continue to compete for IT budgets, particularly within the core ITSM market .
ServiceNow's growth is also exposed to macroeconomic risk. As a significant line item in enterprise IT budgets, sales cycles for its platform can be impacted by a broader economic slowdown, which may cause customers to delay or scale back large transformation projects . Management has already signaled a degree of caution around the U.S. federal sector due to budget tightening, which exemplifies this risk. Finally, as the company grows, the potential for larger, more transformative M&A increases. The pending $2.9 billion acquisition of Moveworks represents a step-up in deal size and introduces greater integration and execution risk compared to its historical focus on smaller, technology-focused acquisitions.

Capital Allocation

ServiceNow maintains a strong and flexible balance sheet, characterized by a healthy net cash position. As of December 31, 2024, the company held $5.8 billion in cash and equivalents against $1.5 billion in debt . By the end of Q2 2025, the total debt-to-equity ratio was a very low 0.22, providing significant capacity for strategic investments and shareholder returns .
The company's M&A strategy has been disciplined and strategically sound. It has historically focused on smaller, "tuck-in" acquisitions of technology and talent that can be natively integrated into the Now Platform, thereby enhancing its capabilities without introducing the integration risks associated with large, revenue-driven mergers. Recent examples that fit this strategy include the completed acquisition of data.world and the announced acquisition of Moveworks, both aimed at bolstering the platform's AI and data management capabilities .
ServiceNow does not pay a dividend . The primary method of returning capital to shareholders is through its share repurchase program. In January 2025, the Board of Directors authorized an additional $3 billion for the program . During the second quarter of 2025, the company repurchased approximately 381,000 shares for $361 million . The stated objective of the program is primarily to manage the impact of dilution from employee stock-based compensation . This prudent approach—prioritizing organic R&D, maintaining a strong balance sheet, executing disciplined M&A, and using buybacks to offset dilution—earns an Exemplary Capital Allocation rating.

ESG Risk

ServiceNow holds an ESG Risk Rating of 15.0 from Sustainalytics, which places it in the "Low Risk" category and indicates strong management of its material ESG issues relative to peers. For an enterprise software company, the most material ESG issues are concentrated in the Social and Governance pillars rather than the Environmental one.
Key material issues include:
Human Capital: The ability to attract, develop, and retain elite engineering and sales talent is paramount to sustaining innovation and driving growth.
Data Privacy and Security: The Now Platform is a critical system of record that processes and stores sensitive customer data. Therefore, robust data governance, stringent privacy policies, and state-of-the-art cybersecurity measures are essential for maintaining customer trust, ensuring compliance, and mitigating regulatory risk .
Corporate Governance: Strong board oversight, transparent business ethics, and sound risk management practices are crucial for protecting long-term shareholder value .
The company's low ESG risk score can be viewed as an indicator of high operational quality. Strong performance in areas like data security and corporate governance are not merely ethical considerations; they are mission-critical business functions. Effective management of these risks is fundamental to the company's long-term success and reinforces the overall quality of the business. It is also noteworthy that Sustainalytics has updated its methodology, effective July 1, 2025, to rely solely on public information, a move that enhances transparency and is unlikely to negatively impact ServiceNow's rating .

Appendix

Glossary of Ratings
Economic Moat: A rating of Wide, Narrow, or None that reflects the sustainability of a company's competitive advantages. A Wide moat indicates advantages that are expected to last for more than 20 years.
Uncertainty: A rating of Low, Medium, High, or Very High that captures the range of potential outcomes for a company's intrinsic value. It is a key input for determining the margin of safety required for an investment.
Capital Allocation: A rating of Exemplary, Standard, or Poor that assesses management's effectiveness in investing capital to create shareholder value. It considers balance sheet health, M&A strategy, and shareholder return policies.
