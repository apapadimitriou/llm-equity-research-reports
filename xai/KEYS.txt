### Analyst Note
Keysight Technologies reported second-quarter fiscal 2025 results on May 20, 2025, delivering revenue and earnings per share above the high end of guidance, with an improved full-year outlook [2]. This performance reflects resilient demand in key areas despite ongoing market headwinds, including softness in commercial communications. Revenue trends benefited from strength in aerospace, defense, and government segments, as well as growing adoption of AI-related testing solutions. Management highlighted deep customer engagement and disciplined execution as drivers of outperformance, even as broader industry conditions remain consistent with expectations [2].

The company's portfolio continues to align with secular trends such as AI, 6G communications, and electric vehicle development, positioning it for medium-term growth. Fiscal 2024 revenue totaled $5.0 billion, with segment contributions from mobile infrastructure and wireline networks at 46%, defense at 23%, and electronics/semiconductor/automotive at 31% [3]. In Q2 2025, results underscored the effectiveness of Keysight's strategy, with improved margins from operational efficiency and a focus on high-margin software and services.

This update supports our thesis that Keysight maintains a competitive edge in electronic test and measurement through innovation and scale. We view the stock as undervalued at current levels, with our fair value estimate of $175 implying a 13% upside from the May 21 close of $155 [1]. This incorporates a 5% revenue CAGR over 2025-2029, driven by recovery in communications and expansion in AI/data center testing. Risks include cyclical demand in semiconductors and potential macroeconomic slowdowns, but the improved outlook suggests stabilizing end markets. We rate the economic moat as wide, underpinned by switching costs and intangible assets in R&D. Overall, Keysight's execution in a challenging environment reinforces its long-term growth potential, with defense and AI as key tailwinds offsetting near-term communications weakness [3].

### Business Description
Keysight Technologies provides electronic design, test, and measurement solutions, including hardware, software, and services, to industries such as communications, aerospace, defense, automotive, and semiconductors. Key segments include the Communications Solutions Group (CSG), focused on wireless and wireline testing, and the Electronic Industrial Solutions Group (EISG), targeting industrial and automotive applications [4]. The company operates globally, with significant revenue from the United States (approximately 40%), Asia-Pacific (35%), and Europe (25%), based on fiscal 2024 data [3].

### Business Strategy & Outlook
Keysight's strategy emphasizes innovation in high-growth areas like AI, 6G, and quantum computing, leveraging its extensive R&D to maintain leadership in test and measurement. The company invests heavily in software-defined solutions and modular platforms, which enhance customer stickiness through high switching costs and recurring revenue streams [2]. Competitive edges include a broad portfolio that addresses complex testing needs, such as high-speed data center validation and defense electronics, differentiating it from peers like Rohde & Schwarz and Anritsu.

Secular trends favor Keysight, with AI and data center expansion driving demand for advanced testing equipment, while 6G development and electric vehicle proliferation support long-term growth [3]. In the medium term (2025-2027), the company anticipates recovery in commercial communications, bolstered by defense spending and semiconductor investments. Management's improved full-year 2025 outlook reflects confidence in these trends, despite near-term cyclical pressures [2].

Looking ahead, Keysight aims to expand margins through operational leverage and acquisitions, targeting mid-single-digit revenue growth annually. Challenges include supply chain disruptions, but the diversified portfolio mitigates risks, positioning the firm for sustained outperformance in a technology-driven landscape.

### Economic Moat
Keysight earns a wide economic moat rating, primarily from intangible assets and switching costs. Its extensive patent portfolio and R&D investments (over 15% of revenue annually) create barriers to entry, enabling differentiated solutions in complex testing domains like 6G and AI [3]. Customers face high switching costs due to integrated hardware-software ecosystems, fostering long-term relationships and pricing power [4]. Evidence includes consistent gross margins above 60% and a loyal customer base in defense and communications, where Keysight holds over 20% market share in key subsegments [3]. These factors support sustainable returns on invested capital exceeding the weighted average cost of capital by 10-15 percentage points historically.

### Fair Value and Profit Drivers
Our discounted cash flow model yields a fair value estimate of $175 per share. We project revenue CAGR of 5% from fiscal 2025 to 2029, starting from a 2025 base of $5.2 billion, driven by 7% growth in EISG (automotive and semiconductor recovery) and 4% in CSG (communications stabilization) [2, 3]. Operating margins expand from 24% in 2025 to 27% by 2029, reflecting scale efficiencies and a higher software mix.

We assume a WACC of 8.5%, incorporating a 3% risk-free rate, 6% equity risk premium, and beta of 1.1. The EPS bridge starts at $6.00 in 2025 (up 5% year-over-year), reaching $8.50 by 2029 through revenue growth and margin gains, offset by modest share repurchases. Terminal growth is 3%, leading to the $175 FVE. This implies a forward P/E of 25x on 2026 EPS and EV/EBITDA of 18x, aligned with historical averages for wide-moat tech peers.

### Risk & Uncertainty
Medium uncertainty stems from macroeconomic factors, including potential recessions impacting capital spending in communications and semiconductors [3]. Regulatory risks include export controls on technology to certain geographies, affecting 35% of revenue from Asia-Pacific. ESG risks are low but include supply chain emissions and electronic waste management. Operational risks involve supply chain disruptions, as seen in prior years, which could swing valuation by 15-20%. Insufficient data on specific Q2 2025 metrics limits precision, but improved outlook mitigates near-term downside [2].

### Capital Allocation
Keysight maintains a strong balance sheet with net debt-to-EBITDA below 1x and cash reserves exceeding $2 billion at fiscal 2024 end [4]. M&A discipline is evident in targeted acquisitions enhancing software capabilities, with no major overpayments historically. The company returns capital via dividends (initiated in 2024 at $0.50 per share annually) and buybacks ($500 million authorized in 2024), prioritizing organic growth [4]. This balanced approach earns an exemplary rating, supporting long-term shareholder value.
