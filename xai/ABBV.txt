### Business Description
AbbVie is a global biopharmaceutical company that develops and markets innovative treatments in immunology, oncology, neuroscience, and other therapeutic areas [2]. Key segments include immunology (products like Skyrizi and Rinvoq for autoimmune diseases), oncology (Imbruvica for blood cancers), neuroscience (Vraylar for psychiatric disorders), and aesthetics (Botox). The company was spun off from Abbott Laboratories in 2013 and has grown through acquisitions, such as Allergan in 2020. Geographically, operations are concentrated in the U.S. (approximately 75% of revenue), with significant presence in Europe and Asia [2].

### Business Strategy & Outlook
AbbVie's strategy focuses on leveraging its R&D pipeline to offset losses from mature products like Humira, which faced biosimilar competition starting in 2023. The company invests heavily in biologics and small molecules, with a competitive edge from high switching costs in chronic disease treatments and strong intellectual property protection. Acquisitions, such as ImmunoGen in 2024 for oncology assets, enhance its portfolio in high-growth areas like antibody-drug conjugates.

Secular trends favoring AbbVie include aging populations driving demand for immunology and oncology drugs, as well as advancements in personalized medicine. However, the industry faces headwinds from drug pricing reforms and regulatory scrutiny.

In the medium term (2025-2027), AbbVie is projected to achieve revenue growth of 4-6% annually, driven by Skyrizi and Rinvoq ramp-up, which are expected to generate combined sales exceeding $15 billion by 2025 [0]. Margins should stabilize around 45% as cost efficiencies from scale offset R&D expenses. Outlook remains positive, though dependent on successful phase 3 trial outcomes and minimal biosimilar impact on newer products.

### Economic Moat
AbbVie possesses a wide economic moat, primarily sourced from intangible assets like patents and brand power in chronic disease treatments [2]. High switching costs for patients on biologics, such as Rinvoq, create barriers to entry, as evidenced by sustained market share in immunology despite competition. Efficient scale in manufacturing and distribution further supports profitability, with operating margins historically above 40%. Evidence includes the company's ability to maintain pricing power, as seen in Q1 2025 adjusted EPS growth of 6.5% amid industry headwinds [3].

ESG risks center on access to medicine and clinical trial ethics. Operational risks include pipeline failures, with over 50% of value tied to late-stage assets [2]. These factors contribute to medium uncertainty, with valuation sensitivity of ±20% to revenue assumptions.
