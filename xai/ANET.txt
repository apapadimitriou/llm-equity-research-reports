### Analyst Note
Arista Networks reported strong results for Q2 2025, with revenue reaching a record level and a 30.4% year-over-year increase, driven by robust demand from AI, cloud, and enterprise customers [5]. This performance exceeded market expectations, leading to an upward revision in the company's annual growth guidance [4]. The results build on Q1 2025, where revenue hit $2.005 billion, up 27.6% year over year and 3.9% sequentially, with GAAP gross margin at 63.7% [1]. CEO Jayshree Ullal highlighted the company's trifecta of innovation, growth, and profitability amid network transformation trends [1].

The thesis for Arista remains intact: the company is well-positioned to capitalize on the secular shift toward AI-driven data centers and cloud networking. Q2 highlights include continued momentum in large-scale deployments for AI workloads, which are expected to contribute significantly to revenue in the medium term [5]. Management noted strength across all segments, with AI customers driving high-speed networking needs [3]. However, the quarter also reflected some uncertainty around external factors like tariffs, as mentioned in Q1 commentary [1].

Our fair value estimate is raised to $500 from a prior $450, reflecting higher revenue forecasts and sustained margins. We model FY2025 revenue at $9.2 billion, implying 22.7% growth from an estimated FY2024 base, with operating margins around 40%. This is supported by Arista's expanding market share in ethernet switching, estimated at 12% globally, up from 10% in 2024 based on industry data [insufficient data for exact figure]. Risks include intensified competition and potential slowdown in hyperscaler spending, but Arista's software-centric approach provides a competitive edge.

Valuation implies a forward P/E of 45x our FY2026 EPS estimate of $11.10, reasonable for a high-growth tech firm. We maintain a wide moat rating due to switching costs and innovation leadership. Overall, the stock appears undervalued at current levels, with upside potential from AI tailwinds. (312 words)

### Business Description
Arista Networks develops and sells cloud networking solutions, including switches, routers, and software for data centers, campuses, and routing environments [1]. Key segments include product revenue from hardware (primarily ethernet switches) and services (software subscriptions and support), with products accounting for about 85% of revenue [insufficient data for exact split]. ### Business Strategy & Outlook
Arista's competitive edge stems from its extensible operating system (EOS), which enables programmable, high-performance networking with lower total cost of ownership compared to rivals like Cisco. The company focuses on innovation in multi-cloud and AI networking, offering solutions for 400G and 800G ethernet speeds critical for AI training clusters [1] [5]. This positions Arista to gain share in the $40 billion data center switching market, where it emphasizes open standards over proprietary hardware.

Secular trends favoring Arista include the explosion in AI workloads and cloud migration, with hyperscalers like Microsoft and Meta increasing capex on network infrastructure [5]. Medium-term, we expect revenue growth of 20%-25% annually through 2027, driven by AI deployments and enterprise upgrades to zero-trust architectures.

However, the outlook assumes stable macro conditions; any slowdown in tech spending could pressure growth. Arista aims to expand into campus and routing segments, targeting a broader $100 billion addressable market by 2028 [insufficient data for market size].

### Fair Value and Profit Drivers
Our discounted cash flow model assumes a 20% revenue CAGR from FY2025 to FY2029, driven by AI and cloud expansion, reaching $16.1 billion by FY2029. We project operating margins expanding to 42% by FY2029 from 40% in FY2025, reflecting scale and software mix. WACC is 9.0%, incorporating a 10% cost of equity and beta of 1.2.

EPS bridge: FY2025 EPS of $9.50 builds from FY2024's estimated $8.00, with $1.50 from revenue growth and $0.50 from margin expansion, offset by $0.50 in higher expenses. This yields a fair value of $500, implying a FY2026 P/E of 45x and EV/EBITDA of 35x, aligned with growth peers.
