## Business Description

The Cigna Group operates as a global health services company providing comprehensive medical solutions through two primary segments. Evernorth Health Services, the larger segment, encompasses pharmacy benefit management through Express Scripts, specialty pharmacy services, care delivery solutions, and health management programs serving health plans, employers, and government organizations [1]. This division represents approximately 30% of the company's income and has emerged as the primary growth driver, leveraging unique capabilities in specialty drug distribution and clinical management [17].

Cigna Healthcare delivers medical and coordinated health solutions to individual and employer customers, operating both domestically and in over 30 countries internationally [1]. Following the March 2025 divestiture of Medicare businesses, this segment now focuses primarily on commercial health insurance plans and international operations, serving 18 million medical customers as of the first quarter [3]. The streamlined portfolio eliminates exposure to the challenging Medicare Advantage market while preserving pharmacy benefit relationships through ongoing service agreements with HCSC [8].

## Business Strategy & Outlook

Cigna's strategic transformation centers on maximizing the value of its integrated healthcare services platform while exiting capital-intensive, regulatory-challenged segments. The company's competitive advantage lies in the breadth and depth of Evernorth's capabilities, particularly in specialty pharmacy where it delivered over 8 million prescriptions to more than 1 million patients through owned operational assets in 2024 [17]. This vertical integration creates switching costs and enables comprehensive patient management that competitors cannot easily replicate.

The secular growth in specialty pharmaceuticals, driven by aging demographics and advancing treatments for complex conditions, positions Evernorth favorably for sustained expansion. Management's focus on biosimilar adoption, exemplified by the strategic removal of Humira from preferred formularies in favor of lower-cost alternatives, demonstrates the platform's ability to drive value for clients while maintaining clinical outcomes [19][20]. The company's $700 million investment in digital health and AI through Cigna Ventures further strengthens its innovation capabilities and competitive moat [18].

Medium-term outlook remains positive as the streamlined portfolio reduces earnings volatility while concentrating resources on higher-growth opportunities. The elimination of Medicare Advantage exposure removes a significant source of regulatory risk and margin pressure, allowing management to focus on expanding Evernorth's market share and developing new service offerings.

## Bulls Say / Bears Say

**Bulls Say:** The Medicare divestiture eliminates a major source of earnings volatility and regulatory risk while generating substantial capital for shareholder returns and growth investments. Evernorth's specialty pharmacy leadership position benefits from strong secular tailwinds and high client switching costs. The company's biosimilar strategy demonstrates pricing power and ability to drive value for customers. Strong cash generation and disciplined capital allocation support sustained shareholder returns.

**Bears Say:** Loss of Medicare scale may reduce negotiating leverage with pharmaceutical manufacturers and limit growth opportunities in the fastest-growing healthcare demographic. Regulatory scrutiny of pharmacy benefit managers could pressure margins and business practices. Competition from integrated healthcare players like UnitedHealth Group and CVS Health may erode market share. High debt levels of $32.0 billion limit financial flexibility and increase interest rate sensitivity [18].

## Economic Moat

Cigna maintains a narrow economic moat based on switching costs and network effects within its pharmacy benefit management operations. Express Scripts' relationships with health plans, employers, and pharmaceutical manufacturers create a multi-sided platform where value increases with scale [1]. The specialty pharmacy infrastructure, including sterile compounding facilities and clinical management capabilities, represents significant barriers to entry that require years to develop and license [17].

However, the moat faces pressure from increasing regulatory scrutiny of PBM practices and vertical integration by competitors. The loss of Medicare scale following the HCSC transaction may modestly reduce negotiating power with drug manufacturers, though the impact should be limited given Evernorth's remaining commercial volume. The company's investments in digital health and AI capabilities help strengthen the moat by improving clinical outcomes and operational efficiency.

## Risk & Uncertainty

Primary risks include intensifying regulatory scrutiny of pharmacy benefit management practices, which could result in margin compression or business model changes. The Federal Trade Commission's ongoing investigation into PBM practices creates uncertainty around rebate structures and vertical integration strategies. Additionally, drug pricing reforms could impact the economics of specialty pharmacy operations.

Operational risks encompass client concentration in government contracts and large employer relationships, where losses could significantly impact revenue. The Department of Defense contract represents a material relationship that requires continuous renewal and performance [18]. Competition from integrated players like UnitedHealth Group's OptumRx poses ongoing market share threats.

Macroeconomic risks include interest rate sensitivity given the $32.0 billion debt burden and potential recession impacts on commercial enrollment. ESG considerations around drug pricing transparency and access could influence regulatory approaches and client preferences.

## Capital Allocation

Cigna maintains a strong balance sheet with total debt-to-equity ratio of 0.78, below the industry average and manageable given consistent cash generation [13]. The company's capital allocation priorities emphasize debt reduction, dividend growth, and opportunistic share repurchases, with the majority of Medicare divestiture proceeds targeted for buybacks [12][16].

The board approved an 8% dividend increase to $1.51 per share quarterly and authorized an additional $6.0 billion in share repurchase capacity, bringing total authorization to $10.3 billion [4]. Acquisition discipline appears improved following the VillageMD impairment, with management focusing on organic growth and strategic partnerships rather than large transformational deals.
