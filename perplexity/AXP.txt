## Analyst Note

American Express delivered robust second-quarter 2025 results, with earnings per share of $4.08 exceeding consensus estimates of $3.86, representing 9% year-over-year growth[1]. Revenue reached $17.86 billion, up 9.3% annually and above the $17.70 billion consensus[1]. The company's premium positioning continues to drive outperformance, with total billed business growing 6% year-over-year to $387.4 billion in Q2[3]. Particularly noteworthy is the strong momentum among younger demographics, with millennials and Generation Z driving 16% spending growth and representing 34% of U.S. consumer spending[7]. Credit metrics remain excellent, with net write-off rates at 1.9% in Q4 2024, below pre-pandemic levels of 2.2%[7][9]. The company maintained full-year guidance for 8-10% revenue growth, supported by continued premium card demand and international expansion[2][7]. Management authorized a 17% dividend increase to $0.82 per share, reflecting confidence in cash generation capabilities[9][12]. With analyst consensus projecting 13.9% earnings growth for both 2025 and 2026, American Express remains well-positioned to capitalize on the premiumization trend in financial services[2]. Trading at a forward P/E of approximately 15.5x, the stock appears reasonably valued given the quality of the earnings stream and sustainable competitive advantages in the premium segment[17].

## Business Description

American Express operates as a globally integrated payments company, providing credit and charge cards, payment and financing products, merchant acquisition and processing services, and travel-related services. The company operates through four primary segments: U.S. Consumer Services, Commercial Services, International Card Services, and Global Merchant and Network Services[16]. Unlike traditional card issuers, American Express functions as both a card issuer and payment processor, operating a closed-loop network that allows the company to capture revenue from both cardholders and merchants. The company's product portfolio spans premium consumer cards including the iconic Platinum and Gold cards, commercial cards for businesses of all sizes, and merchant services that enable transaction processing. American Express serves approximately 118 million cardholders globally, with 56% active in the United States[4]. The company's geographic presence extends across major international markets, with particular strength in premium travel and entertainment segments.

## Business Strategy & Outlook

American Express's strategic focus centers on expanding its premium customer base while leveraging its differentiated value proposition in the affluent consumer segment. The company's "growth of premium portfolio" represents its top strategic imperative, capitalizing on sustained demand for luxury lifestyle services among wealthy consumers[8]. Management expects continued robust performance driven by three key factors: strong engagement from younger demographics, international expansion opportunities, and the premiumization trend in financial services. The company holds approximately 25% market share in global premium credit cards, positioning it to benefit from the structural shift toward fee-based premium products[2][4]. Digital transformation initiatives, including extensive use of artificial intelligence for fraud detection, personalization, and credit risk assessment, enhance operational efficiency and customer experience[16][18]. American Express's closed-loop network model provides competitive advantages through superior data analytics capabilities and direct merchant relationships, enabling more effective customer acquisition and retention strategies.

## Bulls Say / Bears Say

**Bulls Say:** American Express benefits from an unmatched position in the premium credit card market, with a loyal, affluent customer base that demonstrates spending resilience during economic uncertainty. The company's 16% growth in spending among millennials and Gen Z indicates successful customer acquisition in key demographics[7]. Strong credit metrics, with write-off rates below pre-pandemic levels, demonstrate the quality of the customer base and effective risk management[7][9]. The closed-loop network model generates superior unit economics compared to traditional card issuers, while digital transformation initiatives drive operational leverage. International expansion and commercial card growth provide additional runway for sustainable revenue growth.

**Bears Say:** American Express faces headwinds from elevated interest rates, which pressure net interest margins and increase funding costs. The company's premium customer focus creates vulnerability to economic downturns that disproportionately impact discretionary spending. Rising marketing expenses to maintain customer acquisition momentum may compress profit margins[17]. The pending Discover/Capital One merger could intensify competitive pressure in the premium card segment[8]. Valuation concerns persist with the stock trading at elevated multiples compared to historical levels, limiting upside potential[17].

## Economic Moat

American Express possesses a wide economic moat anchored by several sustainable competitive advantages. The company's premium brand positioning creates significant customer loyalty and pricing power, evidenced by successful annual fee increases and strong retention rates. The closed-loop network model generates network effects, as increased merchant acceptance enhances card utility for consumers, while growing card adoption attracts more merchants. American Express's extensive customer data and analytics capabilities enable superior fraud detection, risk management, and personalized marketing compared to competitors[18]. The company's scale in premium segments creates barriers to entry for new competitors seeking to replicate its value proposition. High switching costs for affluent customers who value exclusive benefits, concierge services, and rewards programs further strengthen the company's competitive position. These advantages combine to generate above-average returns on capital and sustainable pricing power across economic cycles.

Key revenue drivers include 6-8% annual growth in billed business, supported by new customer acquisition and increased spending per card member[3][6]. Net revenue margins are expected to remain stable around 10-11%, benefiting from fee income growth offsetting pressure from higher credit costs. Operating leverage should drive 50-100 basis points of annual margin expansion through 2027, with digital transformation initiatives reducing processing costs. The model assumes a 9.5% weighted average cost of capital and 2.5% terminal growth rate. Earnings per share are projected to grow 13-15% annually through 2026, reaching approximately $16.50[2]. At a 19x forward P/E multiple, justified by the quality and predictability of earnings, the intrinsic value calculation yields the $315 target. Key sensitivity factors include credit normalization timing, interest rate trajectory, and competitive dynamics in premium segments.

Rising interest rates increase funding costs and may pressure net interest margins if deposit pricing remains elevated. Credit risk represents a key uncertainty, with normalization of write-off rates toward historical averages potentially pressuring earnings growth. Regulatory changes affecting interchange fees or capital requirements could impact profitability. Competitive risks include increased competition from traditional banks and fintech companies targeting premium customers. ESG-related risks encompass potential regulatory scrutiny of lending practices and pressure to demonstrate sustainable business practices. Operational risks include cybersecurity threats and technology system failures that could disrupt service delivery. Geopolitical tensions and currency fluctuations pose additional risks to international operations. The company's exposure to travel and entertainment spending creates vulnerability to future pandemic-related disruptions or shifts in consumer behavior patterns.

Share repurchase activity totaled approximately $2.8 billion in 2024, with management indicating continued opportunistic buybacks based on market conditions.
