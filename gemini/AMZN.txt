3. Analyst Note

Amazon reported strong second-quarter 2025 results that surpassed consensus expectations on both revenue and profitability, demonstrating the tangible benefits of its multi-year effort to re-architect its fulfillment network . Net sales grew 13% year-over-year (YoY) to $167.7 billion, while diluted earnings per share (EPS) of $1.68 significantly exceeded analyst estimates . The North America segment’s operating margin expanded by 190 basis points to 7.5%, a clear signal that operational efficiency initiatives are creating structural improvements in the core retail business. The advertising segment also continued its impressive trajectory, with revenue growing 22% YoY to $15.7 billion, providing a high-margin tailwind to consolidated results .
Despite these strong results, the market’s muted reaction underscores persistent investor concerns regarding near-term headwinds . The primary issues are a significant capital expenditure cycle projected to exceed $100 billion in 2025 and a deceleration in Amazon Web Services (AWS) revenue growth relative to peers . AWS revenue grew a robust 17.5% YoY to a $123 billion-plus annualized run rate; however, this growth rate trails that of key competitors .
This dynamic presents a narrative disconnect. Management commentary from the Q2 earnings call indicates that AWS growth is not constrained by a loss of demand but rather by physical capacity limitations, particularly in power availability for data centers. The aggressive capital expenditure plan is the direct strategic response to this supply bottleneck, aimed at capturing what the company views as a generational opportunity in artificial intelligence (AI). While this investment cycle pressures free cash flow in the near term—TTM free cash flow declined to $18.2 billion as of June 30, 2025 —it is a necessary step to secure long-term leadership in the AI infrastructure layer. The market appears to be penalizing the company for this strategic investment while underappreciating the fundamental, positive shift in the profitability of its largest business segment. Given the balance between a premium valuation, near-term free cash flow pressure, and substantial long-term growth potential, we maintain our HOLD rating and $245 fair value estimate .

4. Business Description

Amazon.com, Inc. is a diversified multinational technology company primarily engaged in e-commerce, cloud computing, online advertising, digital streaming, and artificial intelligence . The company reports its financial results across three primary segments: North America, International, and Amazon Web Services (AWS) .
North America: This segment encompasses retail sales of consumer products and subscriptions through websites focused on the U.S., Canada, and Mexico. It is the company's largest segment by revenue, reporting net sales of $100.1 billion in the second quarter of 2025 .
International: This segment includes retail sales and subscriptions through internationally-focused websites. It generated $36.8 billion in revenue in Q2 2025 .
Amazon Web Services (AWS): This segment involves the global sales of compute, storage, database, and other cloud infrastructure and platform services to a broad customer base that includes startups, enterprises, government agencies, and academic institutions . While it is the third-largest segment by revenue, generating $30.9 billion in Q2 2025, AWS is the primary driver of the company's consolidated operating profit, contributing $10.2 billion in operating income for the quarter .
Beyond direct online sales, the company generates significant revenue from third-party seller services (which include commissions, fulfillment, and shipping fees), a rapidly growing advertising business, and subscription services such as Amazon Prime .

5. Business Strategy & Outlook

Amazon's corporate strategy is founded on four guiding principles: customer obsession, a passion for invention, a commitment to operational excellence, and long-term thinking . For 2025, these principles are manifesting in several key strategic priorities that are shaping the company's operational and financial outlook.
The central strategic priority is an aggressive investment cycle to establish leadership in AI infrastructure. Capital expenditures are projected to exceed $100 billion in 2025, a significant increase aimed primarily at expanding AWS data center capacity to meet the immense computational demands of generative AI . This strategy encompasses the full AI stack, from developing custom silicon like the Trainium2 chip to enhancing managed services like Amazon Bedrock and deploying end-user applications like the next-generation Alexa+. This investment is designed to capture the ongoing secular shift of IT spending to the cloud, a trend that is being materially accelerated by the widespread corporate adoption of AI.
Concurrently, a key operational focus is the continued optimization of the retail segment's profitability and delivery speed. Having completed a multi-year re-architecture of its fulfillment network to a regionalized model, the company is now realizing structural cost benefits. This strategy lowers the cost-to-serve per unit, which in turn enables faster delivery speeds and supports the core customer value proposition of broad selection, low prices, and convenience. The company plans to expand its Same-Day and Next-Day Delivery services to over 4,000 smaller cities and rural communities by the end of 2025 .
Finally, the company continues to leverage its vast first-party purchase data to grow its high-margin advertising business. This segment creates a powerful flywheel; its high profitability can be reinvested to subsidize lower retail prices and faster shipping, thereby widening the company's e-commerce moat against competitors who lack a comparable high-margin revenue stream .
For its third-quarter 2025 guidance, management projects net sales between $174.0 billion and $179.5 billion, representing YoY growth of 10% to 13% . Operating income is expected to be between $15.5 billion and $20.5 billion .

7. Economic Moat

A Wide economic moat rating is assigned to Amazon, derived from four primary, mutually reinforcing sources of competitive advantage.
Network Effects: The Amazon Marketplace exhibits a powerful two-sided network effect. A vast base of buyers attracts a growing number of third-party sellers seeking access to this audience. This, in turn, increases product selection and pricing competition, which further enhances the value proposition for buyers . This virtuous cycle is strengthening, with the third-party seller unit mix reaching a record 62% in the second quarter of 2025.
Cost Advantages: The company's immense scale provides superior purchasing power and allows for a lower structural cost base than competitors . Its highly efficient, regionalized fulfillment network and proprietary logistics capabilities create a significant and durable cost advantage in e-commerce that is exceptionally difficult for rivals to replicate .
Intangible Assets: This category includes one of the world's most valuable brands, which is synonymous with e-commerce, convenience, and value . It also encompasses proprietary technology in logistics, automation, and cloud computing (AWS), as well as a vast trove of first-party consumer purchase data that powers the high-margin advertising business .
Switching Costs: The Amazon Prime ecosystem creates high switching costs for its more than 300 million global members . The comprehensive bundle of benefits, including fast and free shipping, Prime Video, and other services, "tightly weaves consumers to Amazon," making it inconvenient for them to fulfill their e-commerce needs through competitors .

8. Fair Value and Profit Drivers

The $245 fair value estimate is derived from a discounted cash flow (DCF) analysis, which is appropriate for a company like Amazon that is strategically reinvesting heavily for long-term growth, a practice that can distort near-term GAAP earnings.
The valuation is based on several key assumptions. A Weighted Average Cost of Capital (WACC) of 10.3% is utilized, which is in line with industry benchmarks for large-cap consumer discretionary technology firms . We model a 5-year revenue compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of approximately 9.5%, which aligns with consensus analyst forecasts and reflects a maturation in the retail segment offset by continued strong growth in AWS and Advertising. Operating margins are forecast to expand toward the mid-teens over the next decade, up from the current level of approximately 11.4%, driven by the increasing mix of high-margin AWS and Advertising revenue and structural improvements in retail profitability .
The primary profit drivers for the company are:
AWS Growth & Margin: AWS remains the principal driver of corporate profitability. Continued double-digit revenue growth and operating margins sustained above 30% are critical to the investment thesis.
Advertising Revenue Growth: As the fastest-growing and one of the highest-margin segments, its continued expansion has a direct and significant positive impact on the consolidated profit profile.
North America Retail Margin Expansion: The successful optimization of the fulfillment network is a key lever for overall profit growth, given the segment's massive revenue base.

9. Risk & Uncertainty

A Medium Uncertainty Rating is assigned, reflecting the dynamic competitive and regulatory landscape .
Competitive Risks: Amazon faces intense competition in cloud computing from Microsoft Azure and Google Cloud, both of which are growing revenues at a faster percentage rate and investing heavily in AI . In e-commerce, traditional retailers like Walmart are improving their omnichannel capabilities, while new, low-cost international platforms are creating pricing pressure.
Operational & Financial Risks: The successful and timely execution of the >$100 billion capex cycle is critical. Delays, cost overruns, or an inability to secure sufficient power could prolong the period of depressed free cash flow and fail to alleviate AWS capacity constraints. Furthermore, heavy investments in generative AI may carry lower initial margins than traditional cloud services, potentially weighing on AWS's profitability .
Regulatory & Legal Risks: The company is subject to ongoing antitrust investigations and litigation from regulators in the U.S. and Europe concerning its marketplace practices, Prime membership, and AWS. Unfavorable outcomes could result in significant fines or mandated changes to its business model. Amazon also faces risks related to counterfeit goods, product safety, and fraudulent activity by third-party sellers on its platform.
Macroeconomic Risks: A significant global economic downturn could negatively impact both consumer discretionary spending, affecting the retail and advertising segments, and enterprise IT budgets, which would slow AWS growth .

10. Capital Allocation

Amazon's capital allocation strategy is overwhelmingly focused on reinvesting operating cash flow into high-return growth opportunities rather than distributing capital to shareholders .
The company's primary use of capital is capital expenditures (capex), which are forecasted to exceed $100 billion in 2025 . This substantial increase from prior years is directed primarily toward building AWS data centers to expand capacity for general cloud demand and, more critically, for generative AI workloads. This allocation is a tangible signal of management's long-term strategic focus on capturing future growth in AI and reflects a conviction that the return on dominating the AI infrastructure layer is superior to other uses of capital .
Historically, Amazon has used strategic mergers and acquisitions (M&A) to enter new markets or acquire key technologies . However, during the current intensive capex cycle, large-scale M&A appears to be a lower priority.
Regarding shareholder returns, the company has never paid a cash dividend and has no stated plans to initiate one. While a share repurchase authorization is in place, it has not been actively utilized, as internal investment remains the clear priority .

12. ESG Risk

According to Sustainalytics, Amazon has an ESG Risk Rating of 25.0 as of September 2025, which is categorized as "Medium Risk". The company ranks 379th out of 443 peers in the Retailing industry group, placing it in the higher-risk portion of its industry . Sustainalytics assesses Amazon's exposure to material ESG issues as "Medium" and its management of these risks as "Average" .
The top material ESG issues identified for the company include Business Ethics, which encompasses concerns about anti-competitive practices; Product Governance, related to product safety and the vast third-party marketplace; and Data Privacy & Cybersecurity . These issues align with the regulatory and operational risks detailed previously.
On the environmental front, Amazon is pursuing sustainability goals, including a commitment to reach net-zero carbon by 2040 . The company announced that in 2024, it achieved its goal of matching 100% of its electricity consumption with renewable energy sources, five years ahead of its original 2030 target .
