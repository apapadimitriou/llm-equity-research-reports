The investment banking component within Global Banking & Markets achieved particularly impressive results in 2024, with fees totaling $7.73 billion, marking a 24% increase from 2023[11]. This growth was driven by significantly higher net revenues across multiple areas, including debt underwriting activities that benefited from increased leveraged finance activity, and equity underwriting that saw strength in both secondary and initial public offerings. The trading operations within Global Banking & Markets demonstrated remarkable resilience and growth, with FICC revenues reaching $13.20 billion, representing a 9% increase from 2023[11]. The Asset & Wealth Management segment represents Goldman's strategic pivot toward more stable, fee-based revenues that provide greater predictability and lower capital intensity compared to traditional trading activities. The growth trajectory in this segment aligns with broader industry trends toward fee-based revenue models and provides Goldman with a competitive advantage in capturing assets under management as global wealth continues to expand.

Platform Solutions, while the smallest of Goldman's three segments, represents the firm's efforts to diversify into consumer-facing financial services and transaction banking. Net revenues in Platform Solutions were $2.43 billion for 2024, showing a modest 2% increase from 2023[11].
