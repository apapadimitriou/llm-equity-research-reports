AbbVie's current portfolio spans multiple high-value therapeutic areas, with particular strength in autoimmune diseases, inflammatory bowel disease, dermatology, and hematologic malignancies. ## Market Dynamics and Competitive Landscape

The immunology market in which AbbVie operates has experienced significant evolution, particularly in the inflammatory bowel disease segment where the company has achieved remarkable success. Market dynamics have shifted substantially since Humira's patent expiration, with physicians increasingly prescribing newer mechanisms of action that offer improved patient outcomes[3][10].

Skyrizi's emergence as a market leader in the IL-23 inhibitor class represents a fundamental shift in treatment paradigms for psoriasis, psoriatic arthritis, and inflammatory bowel disease. The drug's mechanism of action, targeting the p19 subunit of interleukin-23, provides more targeted immunomodulation compared to broader TNF inhibition, resulting in improved efficacy and potentially reduced side effects[3][12]. Current market data indicates that Skyrizi has captured approximately 40% of the total prescription share in the biologics psoriasis market, a remarkable achievement for a drug that received its first FDA approval in April 2019[10].

Rinvoq's position in the JAK inhibitor market has proven equally compelling, despite initial safety concerns that affected the broader class. The selective JAK1 inhibition provided by upadacitinib offers oral convenience compared to injectable biologics while maintaining competitive efficacy across multiple indications[3][12]. The drug's expanding label, which now includes rheumatoid arthritis, psoriatic arthritis, atopic dermatitis, ulcerative colitis, and Crohn's disease, provides multiple growth vectors that management expects will drive sustained revenue expansion through the remainder of the decade[10].

The competitive landscape continues to evolve rapidly, with multiple pharmaceutical companies developing novel approaches to inflammatory disease treatment. However, AbbVie's first-mover advantage in several indications, combined with its established relationships with key opinion leaders and robust clinical development programs, has created sustainable competitive moats that are difficult for competitors to breach[8][12].

Biosimilar competition for Humira has intensified significantly throughout 2024, with pharmacy benefit managers increasingly excluding the originator product from formularies in favor of lower-cost alternatives. CVS Caremark's exclusion of Humira from major commercial formularies, followed by similar moves from Optum and Express Scripts, has accelerated the revenue decline but has also provided clarity regarding the timeline for complete market share loss[15].
