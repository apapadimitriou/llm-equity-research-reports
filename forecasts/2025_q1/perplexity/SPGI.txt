## Business Model and Competitive Positioning

S&P Global has evolved from its traditional credit ratings origins into a comprehensive financial intelligence ecosystem that serves as critical infrastructure for global capital markets. The company's business model centers on providing essential data, analytics, and insights that enable financial market participants to make informed investment and risk management decisions[2]. This transformation has been particularly accelerated through strategic acquisitions, most notably the $45 billion acquisition of IHS Markit in 2022, which significantly expanded the company's data capabilities and market reach[13].

The company operates through six primary divisions that collectively form a robust and diversified revenue base. S&P Global Ratings maintains its position as one of the "Big Three" credit rating agencies alongside Moody's and Fitch, operating in what is effectively a regulatory-protected duopoly with Moody's[10]. This segment generated approximately $4.37 billion in revenue in 2024, representing roughly 30% of total company revenue, with operating margins exceeding 60%[10]. The ratings business benefits from significant barriers to entry due to regulatory requirements and the critical role credit ratings play in global capital markets.

S&P Global Market Intelligence has emerged as the company's largest segment, contributing approximately $4.65 billion in 2024 revenue, or about 33% of total revenue[10]. This division provides comprehensive data feeds, research platforms, and analytics tools that compete directly with Bloomberg, Refinitiv, and FactSet. The IHS Markit acquisition particularly strengthened this segment by adding extensive private company data, supply chain intelligence, and commodities expertise that broadens the addressable market significantly.

The S&P Dow Jones Indices business represents one of the most profitable and strategically important segments despite contributing only about 11% of total revenue at $1.63 billion in 2024[10]. This division manages iconic benchmarks including the S&P 500 and Dow Jones Industrial Average, with an estimated $32.4 trillion in global assets tracking S&P Dow Jones indices, including approximately $13.5 trillion benchmarked to the S&P 500 alone[10]. The business model generates recurring licensing fees from ETF providers, asset managers, and exchanges, creating exceptionally high margins with adjusted operating margins approaching 69%[10].

S&P Global Commodity Insights provides specialized data and analytics for global energy and commodity markets, serving clients who require deep expertise in these complex and volatile markets[10]. This division benefits from the company's extensive data collection capabilities and analytical expertise, providing essential intelligence for commodity traders, energy companies, and financial institutions with exposure to these markets.

The company's revenue composition reflects a highly attractive business model with strong recurring characteristics. Approximately 84% of total revenue is subscription-based, with an additional 12% classified as recurring[13]. This creates predictable cash flows and reduces dependence on volatile transaction-based revenues that can fluctuate with market conditions. The remaining revenue comes from transaction-based services, primarily in the ratings business, which tends to correlate with debt issuance activity and market volatility.
