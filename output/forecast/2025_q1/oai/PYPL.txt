Using PayPal’s latest results and guidance, we start from Q1 2024 actuals (Net Revenue $7,699M, OI $1,168M, Net Inc $888M, EPS $0.83 ([www.sec.gov](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1633917/000119312524123690/d831310dex991.htm#:~:text=GAAP%20%20,))) and management’s Q1 2025 EPS guidance ($1.11–$1.13 vs $0.83 year-ago ([www.sec.gov](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1633917/000163391725000017/pypl4q-24earningsrelease.htm#:~:text=1Q%2725%20%20,%241.08))). We assume modest revenue growth (~3–5% YOY) and continued margin improvement.  Depreciation runs roughly $250–260M per quarter (FY2024 was ~$1.032B ([www.sec.gov](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1633917/000163391725000019/pypl-20241231.htm#:~:text=Transaction%20and%20credit%20losses%20,1%2C261))), so EBITDA ≈ OI + $260M. We also factor in share repurchases reducing shares outstanding.  The resulting 2025 Q1 forecasts are shown below:
