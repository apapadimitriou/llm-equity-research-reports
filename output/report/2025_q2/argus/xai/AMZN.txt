### Investment Thesis
Amazon benefits from secular tailwinds in e-commerce penetration and cloud computing, with AWS capturing AI-driven demand. The company's addressable market exceeds $1T across retail, advertising, and cloud services, supported by a network of over 200M Prime members and global fulfillment centers [5]. Key product pillars include consumer e-commerce, third-party seller services, and value-added offerings like advertising (up 18% in Q1) and subscriptions (up 9%), enabling cross-monetization [1].

Margin expansion and free cash flow generation remain core, with operating leverage from fixed-cost scaling in AWS and retail. Amazon returned capital via $10B in buybacks over the past year, though dividends are absent; FCF is expected to rebound as capex normalizes post-AI buildout [Assumption: Based on management commentary; no post-Q1 data available]. Near-term catalysts include Prime Day sales and AWS re:Invent announcements, potentially boosting 2025 revenue by 10-12% [6].

No dividend paid; focus on reinvestment and buybacks, with $10B repurchased in FY24, reducing shares by 1-2% annually [Assumption: Trend continuation]. ### Management & Risks
CEO Andy Jassy (tenure since 2021) and CFO Brian Olsavsky emphasize AI integration and cost discipline in earnings calls, with transparent guidance on capex [2].
• Regulatory risks from antitrust probes, including EU digital markets act compliance, potentially capping 5-10% of revenue [7].  
• Macro risks like inflation and consumer spending slowdowns, impacting e-commerce growth by 2-5% [5].  
• Competitive risks from Microsoft Azure and Google Cloud eroding AWS share, with AI investments key to retention [1].  
• Geopolitical risks including U.S.-China trade tensions affecting supply chains and cross-border volume [6].  
• Currency fluctuations, with 30% of revenue international, exposing to FX volatility [3].

### Company Description
Amazon.com operates a global e-commerce platform, cloud computing via AWS, and digital content services, with brands like Prime, Kindle, and Alexa. Its footprint spans North America (60% revenue), International (30%), and AWS (10%), offering products from retail goods to enterprise software, processing billions of transactions annually across 20+ countries [5].
