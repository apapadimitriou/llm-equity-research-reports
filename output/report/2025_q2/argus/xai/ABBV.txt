### Investment Thesis
AbbVie benefits from secular drivers in biopharmaceuticals, including aging populations and rising demand for treatments in immunology, oncology, and neuroscience. The company's margin profile remains strong, with adjusted operating margins historically above 40%, supporting free cash flow generation exceeding $18 billion annually in recent years [3]. Capital returns are robust, featuring consistent dividend growth (over 10% CAGR in prior periods) and share buybacks, with billions authorized to reduce share count and enhance EPS.

The addressable market spans chronic diseases, with product pillars like Skyrizi and Rinvoq targeting $200 billion-plus in immunology opportunities. Monetization vectors include premium pricing for innovative therapies, expansion into new indications, and value-added services like patient support programs. Supporting data from Q1 2025 show adjusted EPS growth of 6.5%, underscoring successful diversification from legacy products [1]. Near-term catalysts involve pipeline readouts and potential acquisitions to bolster neuroscience and aesthetics segments.

### Company Description
AbbVie Inc. is a global biopharmaceutical company focused on discovering, developing, and delivering innovative medicines in immunology, oncology, neuroscience, eye care, virology, women's health, and aesthetics. Its network includes flagship brands like Humira, Skyrizi, Rinvoq, and Botox, with a footprint in over 175 countries supported by research centers and manufacturing facilities worldwide.
