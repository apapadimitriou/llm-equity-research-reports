### Investment Thesis
Salesforce benefits from secular tailwinds in cloud-based CRM, with AI integration via Einstein enhancing customer engagement and driving upsell opportunities. The addressable market for enterprise software is projected to grow at 10-12% annually through 2030, supported by digital transformation [1]. Core product pillars—Sales Cloud, Service Cloud, and Platform—generate recurring subscription revenue, with value-added services like Data Cloud contributing to margin expansion. Monetization vectors include consumer payments via integrations and new flows in commerce, bolstering free cash flow generation at 25-30% of revenue historically.

Margin profile remains strong, with non-GAAP operating margins targeting 22% in FY26, enabling consistent buybacks and potential dividend initiation [from posts found on X]. Near-term catalysts include AI deal ramps and cross-border volume recovery, positioning Salesforce for mid-teens EPS growth. Capital returns via $10B+ in buybacks over recent years underscore shareholder focus, with net debt low at under 0.5x EBITDA.

### Company Description
Salesforce Inc. operates the world's leading customer relationship management platform, offering cloud-based software for sales, service, marketing, and commerce. Its global footprint spans over 150 countries, with key brands like Sales Cloud, Service Cloud, and Einstein AI. Products focus on enterprise solutions, including data analytics and integration services, serving diverse industries via a subscription model.
