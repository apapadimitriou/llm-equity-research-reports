### Investment Thesis
The Cigna Group benefits from secular tailwinds in healthcare, including aging populations and rising demand for managed care and pharmacy benefits. Its dual-pillar structure—Evernorth (pharmacy and health services) and Cigna Healthcare (insurance)—drives diversified revenue, with 2024 total revenues of $247.1 billion up 27% year-over-year [3]. Margins remain solid at 3.8% adjusted operating margin in 2024, generating strong free cash flow of $11.2 billion, enabling $10.6 billion in share repurchases and dividends [3]. Near-term catalysts include expanded value-added services like behavioral health and potential M&A in specialty care, targeting an addressable market exceeding $1 trillion in U.S. healthcare spend.

Monetization vectors focus on consumer payments via employer-sponsored plans, new flows from government programs, and cross-selling in pharmacy services.
