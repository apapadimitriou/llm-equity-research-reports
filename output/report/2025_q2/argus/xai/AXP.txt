### Investment Thesis
American Express benefits from secular tailwinds in digital payments and premium consumer spending, with its closed-loop network enabling superior data insights and loyalty programs. The company's addressable market includes global consumer payments ($10T+ opportunity) and new flows like B2B, where it captures share through products like virtual cards and working capital solutions [2]. Monetization vectors—card fees (40% of revenue), net interest income, and value-added services—support mid-teens EPS growth, bolstered by operating leverage and low credit losses historically.

Margin profile remains strong at 20%+ operating margins, generating robust FCF ($15B+ annually) for buybacks ($6B in 2024) and dividends (yield ~1.2%). Near-term catalysts include sustained travel recovery boosting cross-border volumes (up 20% in Q4 2024) and potential rate cuts enhancing net interest margins [2]. Thesis hinges on premium customer retention, with attrition below 2%, positioning AXP for outperformance versus broader financials.

### Company Description
American Express operates a global payments network, issuing cards under brands like Platinum and Centurion, with a footprint in over 100 countries. Its scope includes consumer and commercial payments, travel services, and value-added offerings like lounges and fraud protection, generating revenue from fees, interest, and merchant discounts.
