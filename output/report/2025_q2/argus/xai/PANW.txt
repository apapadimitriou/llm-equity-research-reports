### Investment Thesis
Palo Alto Networks benefits from secular drivers in cybersecurity, including rising cloud adoption, AI-driven threats, and regulatory mandates for zero-trust architectures. The company's platform strategy, integrating network security, cloud security, and security operations via products like Prisma and Cortex, addresses a $96B addressable market growing at 15% annually [4]. Monetization vectors include subscription-based NGS offerings, which grew ARR 40% to $4.2B in Q3, driven by multi-product deals and value-added services such as threat intelligence and managed detection [1].

Margin profile remains strong, with non-GAAP operating margins at 28.5% in Q3, supporting robust free cash flow generation of $1.1B, up 20% year-over-year [1]. Capital returns focus on share repurchases, with $500M authorized in Q2, reducing shares outstanding by 2% over the past year [5]. Near-term catalysts include potential AI security partnerships and Q4 billings acceleration, positioning PANW for 15-20% EPS growth in FY2026.

### Company Description
Palo Alto Networks operates a global cybersecurity platform, including firewalls, cloud security via Prisma, and endpoint protection through Cortex. With a presence in over 150 countries, the company serves enterprises, governments, and service providers, focusing on zero-trust security to prevent breaches across networks, clouds, and applications.
