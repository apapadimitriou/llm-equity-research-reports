### Investment Thesis
Morgan Stanley benefits from secular trends in global capital markets, including rising demand for advisory services and wealth management amid wealth transfer to millennials and aging populations. The firm's integrated model—spanning investment banking, trading, and asset management—positions it to capture a larger share of the $10T addressable market in financial services, with product pillars like E*TRADE integration driving consumer payments and new flows [5]. Monetization vectors include value-added services such as prime brokerage and lending, which contributed 15% to Q1 revenues, up from 12% YoY [2].

Margin expansion remains a key driver, with Q1 pre-tax margins at 28%, supported by operating leverage and disciplined expenses. Free cash flow generation of $4.5B in Q1 enables robust capital returns, including $1.5B in buybacks and a 2.5% dividend yield [3]. Near-term catalysts include potential Fed rate cuts boosting trading volumes and a 20% expected rise in M&A activity in 2025, per management commentary [4].
