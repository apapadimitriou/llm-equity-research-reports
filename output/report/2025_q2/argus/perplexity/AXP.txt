## Investment Thesis

American Express operates a unique and defensible business model that combines the roles of card issuer and payment network, creating multiple revenue streams and deeper customer relationships than pure-play networks. The company's premium positioning allows it to command higher merchant discount rates while providing differentiated value propositions through rewards, benefits, and exclusive experiences that drive exceptional customer loyalty. With a 98% member retention rate achieved in 2023, American Express has demonstrated its ability to maintain long-term customer relationships that generate predictable, recurring revenues[12].

The secular shift toward digital payments and experiences-based spending particularly benefits American Express given its strong presence in travel and entertainment categories. The company's strategic focus on attracting millennial and Gen Z customers represents a significant long-term growth opportunity, as these demographics exhibit higher spending rates, stronger engagement levels, and superior credit profiles compared to industry benchmarks. Combined millennial and Gen Z spending increased 15% year-over-year in the first quarter of 2024, with customers aged 35 and under spending 70% more in restaurants compared to other age groups[16].

American Express's capital-light, fee-based revenue model generates substantial free cash flow that supports both growth investments and attractive shareholder returns. The company's disciplined approach to capital allocation, evidenced by plans to increase the quarterly dividend by 17% to $0.82 per share, reflects management's confidence in sustainable earnings growth and cash generation capabilities[5].

## Earnings & Growth Analysis

American Express delivered exceptional earnings growth throughout 2024, with full-year diluted earnings per share increasing 25% to $14.01, significantly outpacing initial guidance ranges. This performance was driven by robust revenue growth of 10% on an FX-adjusted basis, reaching a record $65.9 billion, combined with disciplined expense management and strong operational leverage. The company's diversified revenue streams proved resilient, with discount revenue representing 55% of total revenues, net interest income contributing 22%, and net card fees accounting for 12% of the revenue mix[8].

Card member spending patterns remained strong throughout 2024, with billings growth accelerating to 8% in the fourth quarter, reflecting both consumer and commercial customer engagement during the holiday season. The company's focus on premium customer acquisition yielded impressive results, with 13 million new card acquisitions representing the highest annual total on record. Particularly notable was the continued momentum in attracting younger demographics, with millennial and Gen Z customers demonstrating spending growth of 15% year-over-year in the first quarter of 2024[10].

From a valuation perspective, American Express appears attractively priced relative to payment industry peers. The company's capital position remains strong with ample flexibility to support business growth while maintaining regulatory capital requirements. American Express's unique business model generates substantial free cash flow through its fee-based revenues and network effects, providing consistent funding for dividend payments, share repurchases, and strategic investments. The recent decision to increase the quarterly dividend by 17% to $0.82 per common share demonstrates management confidence in sustainable cash generation and commitment to returning capital to shareholders[5].

Working capital management reflects the operational efficiency of American Express's closed-loop network model. Debt levels remain manageable with a conservative approach to leverage that preserves financial flexibility. The company's access to diversified funding sources, including deposits, securitization markets, and corporate debt issuance, provides multiple options for optimizing cost of capital. American Express's strong credit ratings reflect its stable business model, consistent profitability, and prudent risk management practices that support ongoing access to favorable financing terms.

The dividend growth history demonstrates American Express's commitment to consistent shareholder returns, with the recent 17% increase representing the latest in a series of regular dividend enhancements. Expected share count reduction through ongoing repurchase programs provides additional per-share earnings accretion, with management indicating continued focus on capital return strategies that optimize shareholder value creation.

American Express leadership under Chairman and CEO Stephen J. Squeri has demonstrated consistent strategic execution and strong operational performance throughout various economic cycles. Squeri's tenure, combined with CFO Christophe Le Caillec's financial stewardship, has delivered impressive results including record revenue growth, market share gains in key demographics, and sustained profitability expansion. Management's transparency regarding business performance and forward-looking guidance provides investors with clear visibility into strategic priorities and operational expectations[10].

The company's risk profile reflects both macroeconomic sensitivities inherent in consumer-focused financial services and specific challenges related to its premium market positioning. Economic downturn risks could impact card member spending patterns, particularly in discretionary categories like travel and entertainment where American Express maintains strong market positions. However, the company's focus on higher-income customer segments provides some insulation from broad-based economic pressures, as evidenced by resilient performance during previous economic stress periods.

• Competitive pressure from digital payment providers, fintech companies, and traditional banks could challenge American Express's premium positioning and customer acquisition strategies[9]

• Consumer spending environment shifts, particularly among younger demographics, could affect growth trajectories in key customer segments despite current positive trends[10]

• Credit risk exposure through lending operations could increase during economic downturns, requiring higher provision expenses and impacting profitability[5]

• Technology and cybersecurity risks associated with payment processing and customer data protection require ongoing investments and operational vigilance[15]

• International expansion efforts face currency fluctuations, regulatory variations, and competitive dynamics that may differ from domestic market conditions[5]

• Merchant acceptance challenges in certain regions or categories could limit network growth and customer usage patterns, particularly compared to broader acceptance networks like Visa and Mastercard[9]

Management's approach to risk mitigation emphasizes diversification across customer segments, geographic markets, and revenue streams while maintaining conservative credit standards and robust operational controls. The company's investment in technology infrastructure, data analytics capabilities, and digital transformation initiatives positions American Express to address evolving risk landscapes and competitive challenges effectively[15].

## Company Description

American Express Company operates as a global integrated payments company that provides consumers and businesses with access to products, insights, and experiences that enrich lives and build business success. The company's business model uniquely combines the roles of card issuer and payment network, creating a closed-loop system that enables direct customer relationships and multiple revenue streams. This integrated approach differentiates American Express from pure-play networks like Visa and Mastercard, allowing for greater control over customer experience, pricing strategies, and value proposition development.

The company's product portfolio spans consumer cards, commercial payment solutions, merchant services, and travel-related services, with a particular focus on premium and affluent customer segments. American Express maintains strong brand recognition globally with high acceptance rates in key markets, particularly in the United States where 99% of merchants accept American Express cards. The company's network reaches millions of merchant locations worldwide, supporting diverse spending categories from everyday purchases to large-scale commercial transactions.
