## Investment Thesis

Johnson & Johnson represents a compelling investment opportunity built on three fundamental pillars that position the company for sustained long-term growth despite near-term patent cliff challenges. The company's diversified two-sector structure spanning Innovative Medicine and MedTech provides natural hedging against sector-specific volatility while generating consistent cash flows across economic cycles. With the consumer health divestiture now complete, management can focus capital allocation and strategic resources on higher-margin, faster-growing therapeutic areas where J&J maintains competitive advantages through scale, research capabilities, and global commercial infrastructure.

The Innovative Medicine segment demonstrates particular strength with a robust pipeline featuring over 10 potential blockbuster candidates targeting peak sales of $5 billion each[18]. Key growth drivers include the multiple myeloma franchise anchored by Darzalex, which continues gaining share in earlier treatment lines, and emerging oncology assets like Carvykti in CAR-T therapy. The recent Intra-Cellular acquisition adds meaningful scale in neuroscience with Caplyta's expansion potential in major depressive disorder representing a significant commercial opportunity. Meanwhile, immunology assets beyond Stelara, including Tremfya's inflammatory bowel disease expansion and the oral IL-23 inhibitor icotrokinra, provide multiple avenues for sustained growth.

Johnson & Johnson's financial flexibility enables aggressive capital deployment supporting both organic innovation and strategic acquisitions while maintaining shareholder-friendly policies. The company generates substantial free cash flow exceeding $19 billion annually, supporting dividend growth spanning 63 consecutive years and opportunistic share repurchases. Management's commitment to 5-7% Innovative Medicine growth through 2030 appears achievable given pipeline depth and commercial execution capabilities, while the MedTech segment benefits from secular growth trends in robotic surgery, electrophysiology, and minimally invasive procedures.

## Company Description

Johnson & Johnson operates as a diversified healthcare company through two primary business segments following the 2023 separation of its Consumer Health division into Kenvue. The Innovative Medicine segment develops, manufactures, and markets pharmaceutical products across therapeutic areas including oncology, immunology, neuroscience, cardiovascular, and infectious diseases, generating approximately 64% of total company revenues in 2024. The MedTech segment offers medical devices and solutions across orthopedics, surgery, cardiovascular interventions, and vision care markets, contributing 36% of revenues with products sold globally through direct sales forces and distribution partnerships. Headquartered in New Brunswick, New Jersey, the company maintains operations in over 60 countries with manufacturing facilities, research centers, and commercial organizations supporting a global customer base of healthcare providers, patients, and medical professionals.
