## Investment Thesis

AMD's investment appeal centers on its unique position as the primary alternative to NVIDIA in AI infrastructure while maintaining leadership momentum against Intel in traditional CPU markets. The company's data center business has achieved critical scale with nearly $13 billion in 2024 revenue, positioning it to capture meaningful share of the expanding AI chip market expected to reach $500 billion by 2028[10]. AMD's dual-engine growth model combines accelerating AI GPU adoption with sustained EPYC CPU market share gains, supported by superior price-performance characteristics and customers' desire for supply chain diversification.

The secular shift toward AI workloads creates multiple monetization vectors for AMD across training, inference, and edge computing applications. Management's roadmap through MI400 and MI450 series maintains competitive parity with NVIDIA while offering better total cost of ownership for specific workloads[10][12]. Simultaneously, AMD's x86 CPU business benefits from Intel's execution challenges, with server market share reaching record levels of 33.9% in Q3 2024[11]. This diversification reduces single-point-of-failure risk while providing multiple growth catalysts.

Operating leverage expansion represents a key value driver as AMD scales its higher-margin businesses. Non-GAAP operating margin reached 24% in Q1 2025, reflecting the company's ability to drive earnings growth faster than revenue expansion. Strong free cash flow generation supports continued R&D investment and potential capital returns as the business matures.

## Company Description

Advanced Micro Devices Inc. designs and manufactures high-performance computing solutions including microprocessors, graphics processing units, and related technologies. The company operates through four segments: Data Center serving cloud and enterprise customers, Client providing PC and laptop processors, Gaming supplying console and discrete graphics solutions, and Embedded addressing industrial and automotive markets. AMD's global footprint encompasses research and development facilities across multiple countries with manufacturing partnerships primarily through TSMC for advanced node production.
