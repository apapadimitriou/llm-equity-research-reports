## Investment Thesis

Humana represents a defensive value play in the managed care space, trading at attractive valuation multiples while management executes a strategic repositioning focused on margin improvement over growth. The company's leading Medicare Advantage franchise faces near-term pressure from regulatory headwinds and elevated medical costs, but disciplined pricing actions and market exits should restore profitability to targeted 3%+ margins by 2026. The CenterWell integrated healthcare services platform provides meaningful diversification and higher-margin growth opportunities, with primary care patient volumes up 31.4% year-over-year and expanding geographic footprint through 30 new centers added in the past year[21].

Management's focus on clinical excellence and value-based care delivery positions Humana to benefit from favorable demographic trends as Medicare Advantage penetration continues expanding among the 10,000 Americans turning 65 daily. ## Company Description

Humana Inc. provides medical and specialty insurance products primarily in the United States through two main segments: Insurance and CenterWell. The Insurance segment offers Medicare Advantage plans, Medicaid managed care, commercial health insurance, and specialty products including pharmacy benefits management. The CenterWell segment delivers payor-agnostic healthcare services including primary care, home health, and pharmacy dispensing through an integrated platform serving both Humana members and external patients. Founded in 1961 and headquartered in Louisville, Kentucky, Humana serves approximately 14.8 million members with particular strength in Medicare Advantage where it ranks as the second-largest national provider.
