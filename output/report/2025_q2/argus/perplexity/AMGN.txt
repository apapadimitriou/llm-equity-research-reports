## Investment Thesis

Amgen represents a mature biotechnology leader with diversified revenue streams across four therapeutic pillars: General Medicine, Oncology, Inflammation, and Rare Disease. The company's investment appeal centers on its established market positions, robust cash generation capabilities, and advancing pipeline in high-value therapeutic areas. Following the $27.8 billion Horizon Therapeutics acquisition completed in October 2023, Amgen has expanded its rare disease footprint while maintaining its historical focus on volume-driven growth strategies[1][18].

The secular growth drivers supporting Amgen's long-term prospects include an aging global population driving increased demand for chronic disease treatments, expanding biosimilar opportunities as patents expire across the industry, and breakthrough innovations in areas such as obesity management and cancer immunotherapy. The company's geographic diversification across approximately 100 countries provides natural hedging against regional market volatility, while its balanced portfolio reduces dependence on any single therapeutic franchise[18].

Near-term catalysts include the potential approval and commercialization of MariTide in obesity indications, which could position Amgen as a meaningful competitor in the rapidly expanding GLP-1 receptor agonist market projected to exceed $100 billion globally. Additional clinical readouts across the oncology portfolio, including confirmatory data for accelerated approval programs, represent additional value inflection points through 2025 and beyond.
