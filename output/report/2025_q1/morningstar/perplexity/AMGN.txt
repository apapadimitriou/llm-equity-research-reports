# Amgen Inc. (AMGN) - Equity Research Note

Amgen delivered a strong first quarter 2025 performance with revenues of $8.1 billion and adjusted earnings per share of $4.90, both beating consensus estimates, driven by robust 14% volume growth across its diversified portfolio of biologics. The company's key growth drivers include newer products like Evenity, Tavneos, and Tezspire, while established franchises Prolia and Enbrel continue to generate substantial cash flows despite emerging biosimilar pressures. The highly anticipated obesity candidate MariTide showed promising but not exceptional Phase 2 results with up to 20% weight loss, positioning it as a potential monthly alternative to weekly GLP-1 therapies, though investor expectations may have been overly optimistic. With a robust pipeline, strong free cash flow generation, and strategic acquisitions expanding its rare disease portfolio, Amgen remains well-positioned for sustainable growth, though patent expirations and biosimilar competition present near-term headwinds that require careful navigation.

## Analyst Note

Amgen's first quarter 2025 results demonstrated the company's operational resilience and portfolio strength, with total revenues of $8.1 billion exceeding consensus estimates of $7.95 billion and representing 9% year-over-year growth[1]. The standout performance was driven by robust volume expansion of 14% across the product portfolio, though this was partially offset by continued pricing pressure of 6%[1]. Adjusted earnings per share of $4.90 significantly surpassed the $4.16 consensus estimate, reflecting improved operational leverage with adjusted operating margins expanding 250 basis points to 45.7%[1].

The revenue composition reveals a healthy diversification strategy bearing fruit, with fourteen products achieving double-digit sales growth during the quarter[4]. Notable performers included Evenity with $442 million in sales (up 29% year-over-year), Tavneos generating $90 million (up 76%), and the newly launched IMDELLTRA contributing $81 million in its first full quarter[1][4]. Prolia maintained its position as a key revenue driver with $1.1 billion in quarterly sales, up 10% despite impending biosimilar competition following February 2025 patent expiration[1].

The company's obesity candidate MariTide generated significant attention with Phase 2 results showing up to 20% weight loss over 52 weeks, positioning it as the first monthly-dosed obesity treatment in late-stage development[7][9]. However, the 11% discontinuation rate due to side effects exceeded the 7% rate observed in Zepbound Phase 3 trials, suggesting tolerability challenges that may limit market penetration[9]. Management's strategic pivot toward broader indications encompassing cardiovascular, renal, and hepatic conditions could enhance Medicare coverage prospects and differentiate MariTide beyond dosing convenience[7].

Amgen maintained its full-year 2025 guidance with revenue expectations of $34.3-35.7 billion and adjusted earnings per share of $20.00-21.20[1]. The guidance incorporates current tariff impacts but excludes potential pharmaceutical import tariffs, introducing modest uncertainty. With strong free cash flow generation of $1.0 billion in the quarter versus $0.5 billion in the prior year, Amgen continues demonstrating robust cash conversion capabilities supporting sustained shareholder returns and strategic investments[4].

## Business Description

Amgen Inc. operates as the world's largest independent biotechnology company, discovering, developing, manufacturing, and delivering human therapeutics across global markets since its 1980 founding[3]. The company employs approximately 28,000 people worldwide and maintains its headquarters in Thousand Oaks, California, under CEO Robert Bradway's leadership[3]. Amgen's therapeutic focus spans six core areas: cardiovascular disease, oncology, bone health, neuroscience, nephrology, and inflammation, generating approximately $35 billion in annual revenues[11].

The company's principal revenue drivers include established blockbuster products such as Enbrel for rheumatoid arthritis and psoriasis, Prolia for postmenopausal osteoporosis, and Repatha for cardiovascular risk reduction[3]. The portfolio encompasses both innovative biologics and strategic biosimilars, with newer additions including rare disease assets acquired through the 2023 Horizon Therapeutics acquisition, such as Tepezza for thyroid eye disease and Krystexxa for chronic refractory gout[3]. Amgen serves healthcare providers including physicians, clinics, dialysis centers, hospitals, and pharmacies through pharmaceutical wholesale distributors and direct-to-consumer channels[3].

The company's revenue composition demonstrates geographic diversification with strong U.S. market presence complemented by expanding international operations, particularly in the Asia-Pacific region where volume growth has been substantial[12]. Strategic partnerships enhance Amgen's market reach, including collaborations with AstraZeneca for Tezspire, Novartis for Aimovig, UCB for Evenity, and BeiGene for oncology product expansion[3]. This collaborative approach enables Amgen to leverage partner capabilities while maintaining focus on core competencies in biologics development and manufacturing.

## Business Strategy & Outlook

Amgen's strategic positioning centers on leveraging its biologics expertise and manufacturing scale to maintain leadership in high-value therapeutic areas while expanding into adjacent markets through strategic acquisitions and partnerships. The company's competitive advantage stems from its ability to develop complex biologics with differentiated mechanisms of action, supported by robust clinical development capabilities and global commercial infrastructure[11]. This foundation has enabled successful market share gains through strategic pricing adjustments, as demonstrated by Repatha's 45% volume growth following price reductions that enhanced insurance coverage[11].

The company's medium-term growth strategy emphasizes three key pillars: expanding existing product franchises through label extensions and geographic expansion, advancing a robust pipeline of innovative biologics, and capturing value from strategic acquisitions in high-growth therapeutic areas. The Horizon Therapeutics acquisition exemplifies this approach, adding rare disease franchises that generated $179 million in quarterly revenue with 6% year-over-year growth[1]. These ultra-rare disease products typically command premium pricing with limited competition, providing sustainable margin expansion opportunities.

Secular trends supporting Amgen's outlook include aging demographics driving increased demand for bone health and cardiovascular therapies, growing recognition of biologics' efficacy in inflammatory diseases, and expanding treatment paradigms in oncology through targeted immunotherapies. The obesity market represents a transformative opportunity, with MariTide's potential monthly dosing advantage addressing patient convenience preferences while targeting the estimated $54 billion global obesity treatment market[7]. Management's strategic focus on broader cardiovascular and metabolic indications could expand addressable markets significantly beyond pure weight loss applications.

The company has demonstrated operational excellence by reducing development cycle timelines by 36 months while maintaining industry-leading R&D investment at 17.9% of sales[11]. This efficiency enables faster time-to-market for new products while optimizing development costs. Looking forward, Amgen's pipeline includes promising candidates across multiple therapeutic areas, with particular strength in oncology immunotherapies and innovative delivery mechanisms that could sustain long-term growth beyond current patent expiration pressures.

The company's dependence on U.S. markets for growth creates vulnerability to healthcare policy changes and potential tariff impacts on pharmaceutical imports.

## Economic Moat

Amgen's economic moat derives from multiple interlocking competitive advantages that create sustainable barriers to competition in the biologics market. The company's primary moat source stems from its extensive patent portfolio and regulatory exclusivity periods that protect key products from biosimilar competition for extended periods. Unlike small-molecule generics, biologics face higher barriers to entry due to manufacturing complexity, requiring specialized facilities and expertise that Amgen has developed over decades[15].

The company's manufacturing scale represents a significant competitive advantage, with global production capabilities enabling cost-effective biologics production and supply chain optimization. This scale advantage allows strategic pricing decisions, such as Repatha's price reduction strategy that drove 43% volume growth by improving insurance coverage while maintaining profitability through operational leverage[11]. Amgen's regulatory expertise in navigating complex biologics approval processes provides additional protection, as competitors face lengthy and expensive development timelines to achieve biosimilar approval.

Brand recognition and physician relationships constitute another moat dimension, with established products like Enbrel maintaining market leadership despite theoretical biosimilar availability due to switching costs and physician familiarity[15]. The company's clinical development capabilities enable continuous innovation within existing franchises through new indications and improved formulations, extending product lifecycles beyond initial patent protection. Strategic partnerships with leading pharmaceutical companies like AstraZeneca and Novartis provide access to complementary capabilities while sharing development risks and costs[3].

The rare disease portfolio acquired through Horizon Therapeutics adds ultra-rare disease franchises with inherently limited competition due to small patient populations and high development costs for potential competitors. These products typically maintain pricing power and market exclusivity through orphan drug protections and specialized distribution networks requiring significant investment to replicate.

## Risk & Uncertainty

Amgen faces multiple risk categories that could materially impact valuation and business performance over the investment horizon. Patent expiration risks represent the most immediate threat, with key products including Prolia and Xgeva losing exclusivity protection in 2025, exposing approximately $5.4 billion in combined annual revenues to biosimilar competition[1][13]. Historical precedent from European markets suggests biosimilars can capture 40% market share within several years of launch, as observed with Enbrel's European experience[15].

Regulatory risks encompass both product-specific and systemic challenges, including potential FDA approval delays for pipeline candidates and evolving drug pricing regulations globally. The obesity market opportunity for MariTide faces regulatory uncertainty regarding indication breadth and reimbursement coverage, with Medicare coverage policies potentially limiting addressable market size. Clinical trial risks remain substantial, particularly for late-stage pipeline candidates where development failures could eliminate significant future revenue streams.

Competitive pressures extend beyond biosimilar threats to include innovative therapies that could displace Amgen's products through superior efficacy or convenience. The obesity market's rapid evolution with multiple companies developing next-generation therapies creates execution risk for MariTide's commercial success. Healthcare policy risks include potential changes to drug pricing regulations, international reference pricing adoption, and Medicare negotiation expansion that could pressure margins across the portfolio.

Macroeconomic risks encompass currency fluctuations affecting international revenues, supply chain disruptions impacting manufacturing operations, and potential trade policy changes affecting pharmaceutical imports and exports. The company's substantial debt burden of $53.8 billion creates refinancing risk during periods of rising interest rates, while elevated leverage ratios limit financial flexibility during revenue downturns[16]. ESG-related risks include drug pricing controversies, environmental regulations affecting manufacturing operations, and governance concerns related to executive compensation and board composition that could impact investor sentiment and access to capital markets.

The company's pricing strategies have attracted scrutiny from healthcare policymakers and patient advocacy groups, particularly regarding access to high-cost biologics in emerging markets and Medicare populations. Social impact initiatives include patient assistance programs and charitable access programs that provide medications to underserved populations, though these efforts require continuous expansion to address growing access concerns.

Environmental sustainability efforts focus on reducing manufacturing emissions, water usage optimization, and sustainable packaging initiatives across global operations. Governance practices include board diversity initiatives, executive compensation alignment with performance metrics, and transparency in clinical trial data disclosure.
