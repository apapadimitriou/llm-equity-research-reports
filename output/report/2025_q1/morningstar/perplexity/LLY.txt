## Business Description

Eli Lilly operates as a global pharmaceutical corporation focused on discovering, developing, manufacturing, and commercializing innovative medicines across multiple therapeutic areas[3]. Founded in 1876 and headquartered in Indianapolis, the company operates through two primary segments: Human Pharmaceutical products and Animal Health products, though the latter was largely divested through the Elanco spin-off[3].

The company's human pharmaceutical portfolio spans diabetes and obesity treatments, oncology therapies, immunology medications, and neuroscience products[3]. Key therapeutic areas include endocrinology, where Lilly markets insulin products alongside next-generation GLP-1 receptor agonists, oncology with treatments for breast cancer and other solid tumors, and neurological disorders including Alzheimer's disease research[3]. Geographically, Lilly generates revenue across global markets with significant presence in the United States, Europe, and emerging markets.

## Business Strategy & Outlook

Lilly's strategy centers on leveraging its dominant position in the rapidly expanding GLP-1 receptor agonist market while diversifying across complementary therapeutic areas[12]. The company's incretin franchise, anchored by Mounjaro for diabetes and Zepbound for obesity, targets a combined addressable market exceeding $100 billion annually by the next decade[11]. Management expects tirzepatide-based products to nearly double revenues to approximately $30 billion in 2025 as global expansion accelerates and market share gains from Novo Nordisk continue[5].

The development of orforglipron as the first successful oral GLP-1 therapy represents a strategic inflection point, potentially eliminating manufacturing constraints associated with injectable biologics while expanding patient accessibility[9]. Phase 3 results demonstrated comparable efficacy to injectable alternatives with superior convenience, positioning Lilly for regulatory submissions in late 2025[11]. Manufacturing scalability advantages could prove decisive in capturing market share as demand growth outpaces industry supply capacity.

Beyond incretins, Lilly pursues targeted expansion in oncology through strategic acquisitions including the $2.5 billion Scorpion Therapeutics deal for PI3Kα inhibitor STX-478[13]. The company's radiopharmaceutical investments, including partnerships with AdvanCell and previous acquisitions of Point Biopharma, position it in the emerging precision oncology segment[13]. This diversification strategy aims to reduce dependence on any single therapeutic area while maintaining double-digit revenue growth beyond 2030.
