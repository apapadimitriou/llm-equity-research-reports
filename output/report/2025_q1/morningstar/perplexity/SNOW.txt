## Business Description

Snowflake operates as a cloud-based data warehousing company that provides a comprehensive data platform for storing, processing, and analyzing structured and semi-structured data[9]. The company's platform runs natively on Amazon Web Services, Microsoft Azure, and Google Cloud Platform, offering customers flexibility in cloud provider selection while maintaining consistent functionality across environments[9].

The core product offering centers on a unique architecture that separates compute and storage resources, enabling elastic scaling and pay-per-use pricing that aligns costs with actual consumption[9]. This design allows customers to handle varying workloads efficiently without over-provisioning infrastructure or experiencing performance bottlenecks during peak usage periods.

Snowflake serves customers across multiple industries, including financial services, healthcare, retail, media, and technology sectors[8]. The platform supports various use cases ranging from traditional data warehousing and business intelligence to advanced analytics, machine learning, and emerging AI workloads. As of January 31, 2025, the company served 11,159 customers, including 745 Fortune Global 2000 companies[8].

The business model operates on a consumption-based pricing structure where customers purchase credits that are consumed based on compute resource usage, data storage volumes, and data transfer activities[24]. This approach provides predictable revenue streams while aligning pricing with customer value realization. Professional services complement the core platform offering, helping customers with implementation, optimization, and advanced use case development.
