## Analyst Note

Keysight Technologies delivered strong second quarter fiscal 2025 results with revenue of $1.306 billion and non-GAAP EPS of $1.70, both exceeding guidance[17]. The company demonstrated robust execution amid a gradual market recovery, with orders growing 8% year-over-year and 4% sequentially[17]. Management raised full-year growth expectations to the midpoint of its 5-7% long-term target, reflecting confidence in the demand environment and operational capabilities[17].

The Communications Solutions Group (CSG) reported particularly strong performance with revenue of $913 million, up 9% year-over-year, driven by growth in both commercial communications and aerospace, defense, and government segments[12]. Electronic Industrial Solutions Group (EISG) revenue of $393 million increased 5% year-over-year, supported by semiconductor and general electronics demand[12]. Software and services now represent approximately 36% of total sales, with annual recurring revenue reaching 28% of the mix[17].

Key strategic developments include the pending $1.46 billion acquisition of Spirent Communications, expected to close in Q3 2025, which will strengthen Keysight's portfolio in automated testing and network security[16][18]. The company is also advancing its simulation business through acquisitions and organic investments, targeting expansion in next-generation design and manufacturing capabilities[17].

Financial strength remains robust with $3.1 billion in cash and cash equivalents, while the company has returned over $1.7 billion to shareholders through repurchases over the past twelve quarters[17]. Despite new tariff-related headwinds with $75-100 million annual exposure, management expects full mitigation through supply chain optimization and pricing actions by fiscal year-end[17].

The investment thesis centers on Keysight's technological leadership in test and measurement solutions for next-generation technologies including 5G, AI data centers, and autonomous vehicles. The company's transition toward higher-margin software and services, combined with strong competitive positioning and disciplined capital allocation, supports sustainable mid-to-high single-digit revenue growth.

## Business Description

Keysight Technologies operates as a leading provider of electronic design and test solutions, serving over 40,000 customers globally across communications, aerospace and defense, automotive, semiconductor, and general electronics markets[6]. The company traces its heritage to Hewlett-Packard's test and measurement division, spinning out from Agilent Technologies in 2014 with over 80 years of measurement science expertise[6][9].

The business operates through two primary segments: Communications Solutions Group (CSG) and Electronic Industrial Solutions Group (EISG)[6]. CSG serves the global commercial communications and aerospace, defense, and government markets with electronic design and test software, measurement instruments, systems, and related services[6]. EISG provides test and measurement solutions across automotive, energy, semiconductor design and manufacturing, consumer electronics, education, and general electronics markets[6].

Keysight's portfolio spans hardware, software, support services, and consultative sales capabilities across the entire customer workflow from design through manufacturing, deployment, and optimization[3]. Hardware remains the largest revenue component, though the company has strategically expanded software and services to approximately 39% of total revenue, with pure software representing around 25%[3]. This diversification enhances business resilience and margin profile while establishing recurring revenue streams.

Geographically, the company maintains global operations with significant presence in North America, Europe, and Asia-Pacific regions. The Santa Rosa, California headquarters anchors a network of research and development centers worldwide, enabling local customer support and technology innovation[6]. Manufacturing capabilities include proprietary integrated circuit fabrication facilities optimized for leading-edge measurement performance[6].

## Business Strategy & Outlook

Keysight's strategic focus centers on maintaining technology leadership while expanding into higher-value software and services segments. The company leverages proprietary hardware and software technologies unavailable commercially, developed through global R&D centers to deliver best-in-class measurement solutions[6]. This differentiation enables first-to-market product introductions that help customers accelerate their own innovation cycles and gain competitive advantages[6].

The transition from traditional hardware sales to software-centric solutions represents a fundamental strategic shift. Management is pursuing recurring revenue models through Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR) initiatives, which currently comprise 30% of total revenue[3]. This evolution from the Open System Interconnection (OSI) model's physical layer toward protocol and application layers increases software content and improves business resilience during market downturns[3].

Secular growth drivers include accelerating adoption of next-generation data center architectures, demand for advanced wireline and AI data center technologies, and expanding customer engagement in both electrical and optical domains[17]. The company's early positioning in emerging wireline and data center standards supports market share gains as customers transition digital infrastructure. Additional growth catalysts include 5G network deployments, autonomous vehicle development, and semiconductor manufacturing requirements for advanced node processes.

Medium-term outlook remains constructive despite near-term macroeconomic uncertainties. Management expects gradual market recovery through 2025, supported by robust innovation pipelines and deep customer engagement[11]. The pending Spirent acquisition will enhance capabilities in automated testing, network security, and connected technologies while expanding addressable markets[18]. Strategic investments in simulation and emulation software capabilities align with next-generation design trends and strengthen competitive positioning in R&D-intensive applications.

## Economic Moat

Keysight maintains a narrow economic moat based on technological differentiation and customer switching costs. The company's proprietary measurement technologies, developed through decades of innovation and substantial R&D investments, are unavailable commercially and deliver superior performance for critical applications[6]. In-house integrated circuit fabrication capabilities enable leading-edge instrument performance across core measurement platforms including RF and microwave design, network analyzers, oscilloscopes, and signal analyzers[6].

Customer relationships represent another moat source, with over 40,000 annual customers including most Fortune 1000 companies developing electronic technologies[6]. Deep technical partnerships often span multiple product generations, creating switching costs and preference for integrated solutions. The company's role as trusted advisor in mission-critical applications strengthens customer loyalty and reduces price sensitivity.

Scale advantages in R&D enable continued technology leadership, with substantial investments required to match Keysight's measurement science capabilities. The global installed base of over 1,600 solutions in CSG's communications segment demonstrates market leadership and creates network effects[3]. However, the narrow moat rating reflects competitive pressures from larger technology platforms and the ongoing need for innovation investments to maintain differentiation.

## Risk & Uncertainty

Macroeconomic risks include semiconductor cycle volatility, which affects both direct semiconductor customers and broader electronics demand. Automotive market weakness could pressure EISG segment performance, while communications infrastructure spending fluctuations impact CSG results. Global trade tensions and tariff policies create cost pressures, with current $75-100 million annual exposure requiring ongoing mitigation efforts[17].

Regulatory risks encompass the pending Spirent acquisition, where competition authority approvals remain pending[18]. Export control restrictions could limit growth in certain geographic markets or customer segments. Data privacy and cybersecurity regulations may require additional compliance investments as software offerings expand.

Technology transition risks include customer migration to alternative testing methodologies or in-house capabilities. Platform consolidation in key markets could reduce demand for specialized measurement solutions. Competitive threats from larger technology companies entering test and measurement markets could pressure pricing and market share.

Operational risks include talent retention in highly specialized technical roles, integration challenges from acquisitions, and supply chain disruptions affecting component availability. Foreign exchange volatility impacts international operations, while customer concentration in certain segments creates revenue volatility risks.

ESG risks include environmental regulations affecting manufacturing operations, social responsibility requirements from customers and investors, and governance standards for executive compensation and board composition. The company's net-zero emissions commitment by 2040 requires substantial operational changes and investment[13].

Capital expenditure requirements remain modest, enabling substantial cash flow availability for shareholder returns and strategic investments. The balanced approach between growth investments and returns supports a "Good" capital allocation rating.

## ESG Risk

Keysight demonstrates strong environmental commitment through renewable energy initiatives and operational efficiency improvements. The company's Penang, Malaysia solar installation represents one of the largest industrial projects in the region, generating 18.9 MWh of renewable energy and reducing CO2 emissions by 12,062 metric tons annually[13]. This achievement supports the net-zero emissions target by 2040 and earned recognition through the ESGBusiness Awards 2024[13].

Social responsibility encompasses diversity and inclusion programs, employee development initiatives, and community engagement. The Keysight Leadership Model provides structured development pathways across 100 countries, supporting talent retention and organizational capability building[14]. Workplace safety and employee wellbeing programs maintain high standards across global operations.

Governance practices include independent board oversight, executive compensation alignment with performance metrics, and robust risk management frameworks. The company maintains transparent reporting standards and regular stakeholder engagement through investor relations and sustainability communications.

Compared to technology sector peers, Keysight's ESG profile demonstrates above-average environmental leadership through renewable energy adoption and emission reduction targets. Social metrics align with industry standards, while governance practices meet institutional investor requirements. Material ESG risks include supply chain sustainability, talent retention in competitive markets, and regulatory compliance across diverse geographic markets.

The medium ESG risk rating reflects balanced exposure across environmental, social, and governance factors without significant concentrated risks that could materially impact business performance or valuation.
