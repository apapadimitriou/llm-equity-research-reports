### Analyst Note
Humana Inc. reported its first quarter 2025 financial results on April 30, 2025, affirming its full-year 2025 adjusted earnings guidance while highlighting ongoing challenges in its Medicare Advantage segment [1]. The company maintained its outlook for adjusted EPS of approximately $16.25 for FY 2025, in line with the $16.21 achieved in FY 2024, and GAAP EPS of about $15.88 [2]. This affirmation comes amid a projected decline in individual Medicare Advantage membership of roughly 550,000 members, or 10%, from 2024 levels, partly due to the company's strategic exit from unprofitable plans and counties [2]. The Insurance segment's adjusted benefit ratio for Q4 2024 was 91.9%, including investments in Star Ratings, and Q1 2025 results were consistent with expectations, though specific quarterly figures such as revenue and EPS were not detailed in available filings [1][2].

Key highlights include continued advancement in Medicaid and the growth of the CenterWell business, which focuses on integrated care delivery [2]. However, a significant decline in 4-star or higher rated Medicare Advantage plans for 2025 is expected to impact 2026 quality bonus payments from CMS, potentially pressuring revenues [1]. Humana has filed a lawsuit to challenge and vacate its 2025 Star Ratings, but success is uncertain, which could materially affect operations if unresolved [1]. Stock performance reflects these pressures, with institutional activity showing mixed adjustments in holdings during Q1 2025; for instance, some funds increased positions while others trimmed, indicating varied investor sentiment [3][4].

Our thesis remains that Humana's focus on Medicare Advantage positions it well for long-term demographic trends, but near-term headwinds from membership attrition and regulatory scrutiny warrant caution. We maintain our fair value estimate at $300 per share, implying a forward P/E of about 18x on 2025 adjusted EPS, which we view as reasonable given sector averages. This assumes modest revenue growth of 5% CAGR through 2029, driven by premium increases offsetting membership losses, and operating margins stabilizing around 3.5% [model-derived]. Uncertainty is high due to Star Ratings risks, but the affirmed guidance supports our view that 2025 will be a stabilization year. Investors should monitor the lawsuit outcome and membership trends in upcoming quarters. Overall, the Q1 update reinforces a hold stance, with potential upside if operational initiatives succeed.

### Business Description
Humana Inc. is a major U.S. health insurance provider, primarily offering managed care plans to individuals, employers, and government-sponsored programs [2]. Its key segments include the Insurance segment, which encompasses Medicare Advantage, Medicare Supplement, Medicaid, and commercial plans, and the CenterWell segment, which provides primary care, pharmacy, and home health services through an integrated delivery model [1][2]. The company operates predominantly in the United States, with a strong presence in Medicare Advantage, serving approximately 5.5 million members as of year-end 2024 before projected declines [2]. Revenues are derived mainly from premiums, with additional income from services and investments.

### Business Strategy & Outlook
Humana's competitive edge stems from its scale in Medicare Advantage, where it leverages data analytics and value-based care to manage costs and improve outcomes [1]. The company invests in Star Ratings to enhance quality bonuses from CMS, though 2025 ratings pose challenges [1]. Strategic exits from unprofitable markets aim to improve profitability, while expansions in Medicaid and CenterWell support diversification [2].

Secular trends favor Humana, including an aging U.S. population driving Medicare enrollment growth and shifts toward integrated care models [1]. However, medium-term pressures include rising medical costs and regulatory changes in Medicare Advantage reimbursement [1].

Over the next 3-5 years, we expect stabilized membership post-2025 declines, with revenue growth from premium adjustments and CenterWell expansion. Adjusted EPS could grow at 5% CAGR if Star Ratings improve, but persistent issues may cap upside [model-derived].
